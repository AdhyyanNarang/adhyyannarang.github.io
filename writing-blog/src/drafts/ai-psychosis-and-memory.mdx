**The main question I am trying to answer here** 

What is the mechanism through which humans shape LLM behavior at inference time?

**The context of the question**

The central hypothesis of a lot of my research is that the systems around AIs are under-rated for a couple of reasons:
1. These systems influence the behavior of models through a number of causal directions (a) pre-training e.g Claude is becoming anxious (b) post-training via human feedback (c) post-training via data poisoning attacks
(d) deployment-time via simulator-steering 

2. Even if the model is well-behaved, the impact of AIs on humans is mediated through these systems; for instance, we can look at gradual disempowerment as an example of how seemingly well-intentioned AIs may acquire an undesirable amount of power and disempower humans. 

Re 1: For each of these, we firstly should acknowledge that the influence exists; and then think about whether the influence is desirable or not, and how we can mitigate (or absorb) the undesirable influences.

A lot of these influences have been studied from an adversarial lens; and this is pretty common in the safety literature already. But even well-intentioned humans may have inadvertant negative impact on the system. I am more interested in these types of *naturaly occuring* impacts.
