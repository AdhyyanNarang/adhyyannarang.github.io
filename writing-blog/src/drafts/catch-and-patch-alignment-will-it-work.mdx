The field of alignment has undergone a transition in the last few years. Originally, the field was dominated by theoretical arguments about decision theory and agency. And now the field has become very empirical, what I term the "catch-and-patch" era of alignment: where you use monitors (behavioral and chain-of-thought based) to detect any alignment issues and you patch it using standard techniques (most commonly some type of fine-tuning). Right now, the CoT seems quite reliable, and we are able to reliably detect most alignment failures. But, will this approach work as we head into AGI?

In this post, I take a step back and do my best at breaking down this question using a systems-theory lens. 

## Different possible end states

Friendly AI

Rogue AI

Mixed AI

Mixed AI is unlikely to be an equilibrium point of the dynamics because the failures that appear will likely get patched. Either these patches work well, in which case, we will move to the friendly AI. Or they do not work well and we move towards the Rogue AI.

## Personal Hot Takes

Personally, I do think that the Optimist's perspective which does not take the destabilizing forces into account is a bit naive. On the other extreme, I think that the Pessimist's perspective, which claims that there are no stabilizing forces at all, and all alignment research is making the underlying problem worse, is unlikely to be true.

We are likely to be in some intermediate regime. In the easy version of this, the current catch-and-patch alignment techniques are actually stabilizing and are able to outpace the destabilizing forces.

In the harder version, we would need something like reliable interpretability that we can train against, which is not possible for the model to game.  

In either case, it is a good idea to:
1. Try to limit and slow down the destabilizign forces (even if we are not able to stop them completely). The next post will dive into this in more detail.
2. Adjust the incentive structures so that the stabilizing forces are in line with economic incentives, rather than fighting against them.
